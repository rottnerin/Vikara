# Vikara Design Context

## Design Context

### Users
School principals and administrators evaluating whether to hire Vikara as a consultancy. Teachers curious about PBL and student-centered learning. Students occasionally landing here. Conference attendees following up after a talk. Context: professional but inspired — they've likely just heard a pitch or seen a reference. They're deciding if this is worth their time and school budget.

### Brand Personality
Future-focused, inspired, transformational. The site should feel like stepping into something that already knows where education is going — not a sales pitch, but a conviction. Three words: **bold, alive, purposeful**.

### Aesthetic Direction
Warm earthy palette (cream, deep forest green, teal accent, copper warm tones). Bebas Neue for display/headlines, Crimson Pro for body/serif moments, DM Sans for UI text. Both light and dark themes supported.

Anti-references: bland corporate ed-tech, AI slop (purple-to-blue gradients, cyan-on-dark, glassmorphism everywhere), generic card grids, anything that looks like it came out of a Notion template.

References: editorial confidence, something that feels designed with intention — not a SaaS dashboard, more like a manifesto with good taste.

### Design Principles
1. **Conviction over caution** — every design choice should feel deliberate, not safe
2. **Warmth with edge** — the palette is earthy but the typography is bold; don't let it go soft
3. **Motion should feel alive, not decorative** — animations serve the message, not the other way around
4. **No redundancy** — every element earns its place; no repeated headers, no restating what's already visible
5. **The student is the hero** — imagery and framing always centers the learner, not the consultant
